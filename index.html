IEEE-NUS

An engineering-centric Hackathon to gather passionate students to use technology & programming to solve real world issues, forms teams to work together for 24 hours and substantiate their ideas. We invite participation from undergraduates and up to second year graduate students on a national level, specifically first-year Engineering Students.

About HAX 2017

Conventional hackathon primarily focuses on software, for our hackathon we put an emphasis on hardware, hence the theme “smart wearables”. Understanding that completing hardware fabrication within 24 hours is challenging, contestants are not required to create a completed wearable to score well. Rather, they need to complete either its software, hardware, or design (e.g. CAD), and then show how they can be implemented together.

Continuous support/assistance will be made available for participants. Event for participants include a 24-hour hack and six hour-long “skills and tips” workshops within duration of the hack. Mentors will wander around the venue assisting groups, should any meet technical difficulty.

Assessment criteria is based upon Task Fulfillment, and two of the following three criteria (with unequal weightage): Creativity, Practicality, Rigour. The rubric will be made open to all participants. Afterwards, top performing projects will be showcased/presented to all participants.
